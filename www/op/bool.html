Boolean operators

These are the logical Boolean operators. The punctuation names are borrowed from JavaScript, and the spelled-out words are from SQL. You can use them interchangeably.

 You can use the AND and OR anywhere, but this is where they come from.
Notes
  • The &&/AND and ||/OR operators are "short circuit", meaning they skip evaluation of the right operand if the left is sufficient to determine the result. If the right expression could cause errors or side-effects, "short circuit" evaluation can avoid that. It's pretty rare that you need that though; mostly its just a minor optimization — maybe try to put your more complex conditions on the right.
  • The AND keyword is also used as part of the BETWEEN operator. If there's any risk of confusion, use parentheses.
